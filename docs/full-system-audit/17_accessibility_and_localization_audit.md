# Accessibility and localization audit

English, Spanish, and Korean are active targets. Both localization plan and rollout gates passed. User-facing copy must continue through localization files, while Edge Functions return stable codes rather than raw provider text. Translation must never change authoritative offer facts.

The checked public business form had recognizable labels and legal consent controls. The mobile website checker nevertheless found the form too low and its jump missing (F-008), affecting keyboard/small-screen access as well as conversion.

## Required manual matrix

- EN/ES/KO on smallest supported phones and large text sizes.
- Screen-reader labels/order for auth, browse, deal, claim, redemption, billing, account deletion, and business setup.
- Dynamic Type without clipped facts/actions; Korean wrapping and Spanish expansion.
- Contrast, visible focus, reduced motion, non-color status cues, touch targets, keyboard navigation.
- Native poster/ad renderer readability and fact preservation in every language.
- Voice compose consent/errors and a non-voice alternative.

Automated localization gates do not establish accessibility compliance. Assistive-technology and device testing remain blocked.
